## What is Comic Bundle Finder?

Comic Bundle Finder searches eBay for the issues on your want list and identifies sellers who carry multiple issues you need. That way you can buy several issues from one seller and save on shipping.

## Doesn't eBay already have this feature?

No. Well...no. Say you're looking for 10 issues. You can go to ebay.com and search for the first one on your list, click the first listing, click "Seller's other items", and search through their inventory for each of the other 9 issues, one by one. Then you move on to the next listing. And the next one.

If you play any trading card games like Pokemon or Magic: The Gathering, you might be familiar with TCGplayer's cart optimization tools, which are similar to what CBF does. TCGplayer is owned by eBay, but for whatever reason the functionality doesn't exist on eBay.

## How do I use it?

Paste your want list into the text box on the home page — one issue per line — then click **Find Bundle Deals**. The tool searches eBay for each issue and groups results by seller. Only sellers with 2 or more of your issues appear in the results, unless you specifically search for one single issue, in which case only sellers with 2 or more copies will appear.

## What file formats can I upload?

The tool accepts three formats:

- **League of Comic Geeks (.xlsx)** — export your wish list from your profile page
- **CLZ Comics (.csv)** — export your want list from the CLZ app
- **Plain Excel (.xlsx), comma-separated values (.csv), text (.txt)** — one issue title per line

## Why are some issues missing from the results?

A few possible reasons:

- No active eBay listings matched that issue title
- The issue title format didn't match eBay's listings closely enough — try adjusting the title (e.g. removing the year, or shortening the series name)

## Are the eBay links affiliate links?

Yes, links to eBay listings use the eBay Partner Network. If you buy through a link, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. This helps keep the site running.

## Is my want list stored anywhere?

No. Your want list is only used during your session to run the eBay search. The only time data is saved is when you use the Save/Email results options, and nothing is ever shared with third parties.

## Can I search for trade paperbacks or omnibuses?

The tool works best with single-issue searches, but you can try entering a trade or omnibus title — it'll search eBay for whatever you type. Results may be less consistent for collected editions.

## How often is pricing data updated?

Prices are fetched live from eBay every time you search. There's no cached pricing data — what you see reflects eBay's current listings at the time of your search.
